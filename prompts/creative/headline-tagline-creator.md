# Headline and Tagline Creator

## Description

Create compelling headlines and memorable taglines that capture attention and communicate value instantly. This prompt helps marketers, copywriters, and content creators develop powerful messaging for advertising, landing pages, product launches, and brand campaigns.

## Use Cases

- Landing page headlines that improve conversion rates
- Advertising headlines for campaigns across channels
- Brand taglines and slogans for positioning
- Email subject lines that boost open rates
- Blog post and article headlines that drive clicks
- Product launch messaging and campaign themes

## Prompt

```text
You are an expert copywriter specializing in headlines and taglinesâ€”the kind that make people stop scrolling and start paying attention. Create compelling headlines/taglines based on the following brief:

**Project Type:** [HEADLINE/TAGLINE/BOTH]
**Purpose:** [LANDING PAGE/AD CAMPAIGN/BRAND SLOGAN/EMAIL SUBJECT/BLOG POST/PRODUCT LAUNCH]
**Brand/Product:** [NAME AND BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
**Target Audience:** [WHO YOU'RE SPEAKING TO]

**Key Message:**
[THE ONE THING YOU WANT TO COMMUNICATE]

**Value Proposition:**
[WHAT MAKES THIS OFFERING UNIQUE OR VALUABLE]

**Tone:** [PROFESSIONAL/PLAYFUL/URGENT/INSPIRATIONAL/BOLD/WITTY/EMOTIONAL]

**Emotional Hook:** [WHAT FEELING SHOULD THIS EVOKE?]
Options: curiosity, fear of missing out, excitement, relief, aspiration, belonging, urgency

**Constraints:**
- Character limit: [IF ANY]
- Must include: [ANY REQUIRED WORDS OR PHRASES]
- Avoid: [WORDS OR APPROACHES TO SKIP]

**Inspiration/Direction:**
[ANY EXAMPLES YOU LIKE OR DIRECTION TO FOLLOW]

Please provide:
1. 10 headline/tagline options across different approaches:
   - 2-3 benefit-focused (what the customer gains)
   - 2-3 curiosity-driven (makes them want to learn more)
   - 2-3 emotional/aspirational (connects to deeper desires)
   - 2-3 bold/provocative (challenges or surprises)
2. For each option, briefly explain why it works
3. Recommend your top 3 with reasoning
4. Suggest A/B testing pairs
```

## Example Usage

**Input:**

```text

**Project Type:** Both headline and tagline
**Purpose:** Landing page for a new product launch
**Brand/Product:** FocusFlow - an AI-powered productivity app that blocks distracting websites and suggests optimal work schedules based on your energy patterns
**Target Audience:** Knowledge workers, developers, and creators who struggle with digital distractions and want to do deep work

**Key Message:**
Your best work happens when distractions disappear and your schedule matches your energy.

**Value Proposition:**
Unlike simple website blockers, FocusFlow learns your personal productivity patterns and actively helps you work when you're at your best.

**Tone:** Bold and confident, but not corporate. Speaks like a smart friend who figured something out.

**Emotional Hook:** Relief (from constant distraction guilt) and aspiration (being someone who does great work)

**Constraints:**
- Character limit: Headlines under 70 characters, tagline under 50 characters
- Must include: Reference to focus or distraction in at least half

1. 10 headline/tagline options across different approaches
2. For each option, briefly explain why it works
3. Recommend your top 3 with reasoning
4. Suggest A/B testing pairs
```

## Tips

- **Write 20 to find 1:** The best headlines rarely come firstâ€”generate many options and let the strongest emerge
- **Test the "so what" test:** Read your headline and ask "so what?"â€”if there's not an immediate compelling answer, revise
- **Use power words strategically:** Words like "finally," "secret," "proven," "instant," and "free" work because they trigger emotional responses
- **Match the stage of awareness:** High-awareness audiences respond to direct offers; low-awareness audiences need curiosity hooks
- **Keep it speakable:** Great headlines sound natural when read aloudâ€”avoid awkward phrasing that trips up the tongue
- **Consider the context:** A LinkedIn headline needs to feel different from a TikTok captionâ€”platform voice matters
